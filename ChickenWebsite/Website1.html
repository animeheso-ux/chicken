Facts about chickens!

History

Chickens are domesticated descendants of the red junglefowl from Southeast Asia,
with domestication dating back at least 3,000 years. There are currently more than 28-34 billion chickens worldwide,
outnumbering humans by a significant margin.

Reproduction and Eggs

Hens can lay over 300 eggs per year, and occasionally lay two eggs in a single day.
Egg color is linked to the hen’s earlobes: white earlobes produce white eggs, red earlobes produce brown eggs,
and some breeds even lay blue, green, or speckled eggs. The egg-laying process takes about 24-26 hours per egg.

Unique Traits

Chickens can dream during REM sleep, exhibit self-control by delaying gratification,
and have ultraviolet vision, allowing them to see wavelengths invisible to humans.
Some hens occasionally lay tiny “fairy eggs” with no yolk. Chickens are omnivores and can survive 90% of predator attacks in natural environments.

Physical Abilities

Chickens can run at speeds up to 9 mph (14 km/h) and can fly short distances, with the longest recorded flight lasting about 13 seconds and
covering roughly 90 meters. They do not sweat and cool themselves by panting, drinking water, or flapping their wings.
A rooster's crow can reach around 130 decibels, and chickens can detect light flicker over 100 Hz,
which appears steady to humans.
